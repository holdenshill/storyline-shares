The Main Topic
Hope-Empowered
Leadership
To lift up the name of Jesus
by showing leaders how hope in Christ
shapes the way they lead and live.
Every chapter, every speech, every story we build from here answers to this main topic.

Facets

Facet One
Prayer
Everything begins and ends with prayer. Hope-empowered leaders lead from their knees first — before the board meeting, before the hard conversation, before the vision is cast.
Facet Two
Leadership
Hope in Christ changes how you make decisions, hire people, guard culture, and walk into rooms you can’t yet see the outcome of. It’s how you lead when the numbers haven’t caught up to the calling.
Facet Three
Endurance
Hope is what sustains a mission through change, pressure, and leadership transitions. It’s how a faith-grounded organization lasts — how it resists drift and hands off the flame without dropping it.
Facet Four
Personal Life
Hope isn’t just how you lead at work — it’s how you live, love your family, steward your time, and keep your own soul from drifting. Who you are at home is who you are in the room.
The Thread
Hope is the topic. Prayer, leadership, endurance, and personal life are how hope shows up — in the boardroom, in the hallway, in the long game, and in the quiet places no one sees.

Where We’re At
A quick recap from our April 20 workshop — and what comes next.
What we settled
  • • The umbrella: Hope-Empowered Leadership.
  • • Four starting facets: Prayer, Leadership, Endurance, Personal life.
  • • Audience: Christian leaders in all walks of life.
  • • Format: Scripture-grounded, story-illustrated.
What’s Next
  • 1. Speech first, chapter second. You write a speech for each facet at home.
  • Each speech/chapter should include a scripture anchor, a personal or external story that illustrates it (good example, warning example, or funny example!), and a clear takeaway.
  • 2. Carol helps pull stories and content from her binder.
  • 3. You pass the finished speech to me.
  • 4. I shape it into a chapter outline for the book we can build on.
  • 5. Repeat for the other three facets — and add any that emerge.
