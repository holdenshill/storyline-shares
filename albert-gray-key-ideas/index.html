Session 2 Prep

Key Ideas for Discussion

How Do You Live With Hope?

Below are 18 key ideas drawn from your recent emails and speaking. Each one connects to the core theme of living with extraordinary hope. For each idea, there are three questions: 1. A story you've lived or seen. 2. A lesson you've learned. 3. A Scripture to stand on.

Idea 01
Iron Sharpens Iron

God uses the sharpening of friends, peers, and mentors to cut away negativity and cynicism and build us up in Christ with hope and optimism.

Story

Who is one of the people who has sharpened you most in your life? What did they say or do, and how did it change you?

Lesson

How do you find and embrace sharp iron instead of surrounding yourself with people who only tell you what you want to hear?

Scripture

Proverbs 27:17 — "As iron sharpens iron, so one person sharpens another."

Idea 02
Love God, Love People

When we choose love as our outward expression to God and others, even when we don't feel like it, it transforms who we are on the inside.

Story

Tell me about a time you chose to love someone when you wanted to be angry or bitter. What happened?

Lesson

How does choosing love when you don't feel it actually change who you are on the inside?

Scripture

Matthew 22:37-39 — "Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind… Love your neighbor as yourself."

Idea 03
Do Your Best as Unto the Lord

Treating every task, small, invisible, or thankless, as an offering to God fills even mundane work with meaning and hope.

Story

Tell me about someone at Bethany who did invisible, thankless work as if God Himself were watching. What did their faithfulness look like?

Lesson

How do you keep giving your best when no one notices and nothing seems to change?

Scripture

Colossians 3:23 — "Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters."

Idea 04
Do What You're Supposed to Be Doing

Trusting that God has prepared specific good works for your life in advance gives you a purpose that circumstances can't take away.

Story

When did you discover what God had prepared for you to do? Was there a moment it became clear, or did it unfold slowly?

Lesson

How do you keep doing what you're supposed to be doing when it's hard and the results aren't visible?

Scripture

Ephesians 2:10 — "For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do."

Idea 05
Grace Before Works

Knowing that your salvation is a gift, not something you earn, frees you from the performance trap and anchors your hope in what Christ has done, not what you've accomplished.

Story

When did you catch yourself working to earn God's love instead of working because you already had it? What was happening in your life?

Lesson

How does understanding grace change the way you work and the way you measure success?

Scripture

Ephesians 2:8-9 — "For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith — and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God — not by works, so that no one can boast."

Idea 06
Sacred Trust

Recognizing that what you've been given is not yours but God's changes how you carry it, whether it's a job, ministry, business, or family.

Story

When did Bethany stop being a job and start being a sacred trust? What was the moment you felt the weight of it?

Lesson

How do you carry responsibility as a trust from God instead of a weight on your shoulders?

Scripture

What biblical story or principle speaks to the idea of sacred trust?

Idea 07
Obedience Before a Plan

Stepping into what God calls you to before you have the resources, the strategy, or the certainty builds a life of faith in him instead of in yourself.

Story

When did you obey God without a plan? What did you step into, and what did He do after you stepped?

Lesson

What have you learned about the relationship between obedience and hope?

Scripture

What biblical example of obedience before a plan speaks to you most?

Idea 08
One Life Impacts Another

Living with hope is contagious. The faith of one person overflows to the people around them and to generations after them, which means your hopefulness today is producing fruit you may never see.

Story

Whose faith infected yours? Tell me about the person whose hope overflowed into your life and changed how you see the world.

Lesson

How does knowing your hope is overflowing to others change how you live today?

Scripture

What passage or biblical story illustrates one life of faith overflowing into another?

Idea 09
God Provides

Choosing to trust God for provision rather than relying solely on your own effort opens the door to things you couldn't have engineered yourself.

Story

Tell me about a time God provided something you couldn't have made happen on your own. What were the specifics?

Lesson

What did that provision teach you about trust that you couldn't have learned any other way?

Scripture

What Scripture or biblical story about God's provision means the most to you?

Idea 10
When Nothing Makes Sense, Stay

The decision to stay with Jesus when life doesn't make sense is the defining act of hope. When the crowd leaves, when the answers don't come, when the logical move is to quit.

Story

Tell the story of a time you stayed even when you wanted to leave. What were you facing, and what happened because you stayed?

Lesson

What does staying teach you about hope that leaving never could?

Scripture

What passage or biblical story speaks to the decision to stay when nothing makes sense?

Idea 11
Follow for Who He Is, Not What He Does

People who follow Jesus only for what He gives them will leave when He asks something hard, but people who follow Him for who He is find a hope that outlasts any circumstance.

Story

When did you have to choose between following Jesus for His blessings and following Him for Himself? What did that moment look like?

Lesson

What's the difference between a hope built on circumstances and a hope built on the person of Christ?

Scripture

What Scripture draws the line between following Jesus for what He gives and following Him for who He is?

Idea 12
Faith Is Tested in the Unknown

Sickness, loss, and unanswered prayer are not signs that God has abandoned you. They're the proving ground where hope either becomes real or stays theoretical.

Story

Tell me about a time your faith was tested by something you couldn't explain or fix. What happened?

Lesson

How did that test make your hope real instead of just something you believed in?

Scripture

What passage or biblical story about faith tested in the unknown speaks to your experience?

Idea 13
"I Don't Understand, But I'm Not Leaving"

Peter's answer to Jesus, "Where else would we go?", is the prayer of anyone who has decided that hope in Christ is the only option, even when nothing else makes sense.

Story

When did you pray that prayer literally? What were you facing, and what were you refusing to walk away from?

Lesson

What did staying through confusion teach you about who God is?

Scripture

John 6:68 — "Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life."

Idea 14
Abide, Don't Strive

The secret to sustaining hope over decades is not working harder but staying connected to Christ. Apart from the vine, even the most driven person produces nothing that lasts.

Story

When did you try to bear fruit on your own and burn out? What was happening, and what brought you back to abiding?

Lesson

What does abiding look like in your daily life, and what happens when you stop?

Scripture

John 15:4-5 — "Remain in me, as I also remain in you. No branch can bear fruit by itself; it must remain in the vine. Neither can you bear fruit unless you remain in me."

Idea 15
You Are an Instrument, Not the Source

The moment you realize that God is the one doing the work through you, and you couldn't perform what He has done, is the moment pride dies and unexplainable hope is born.

Story

When did you realize you were the instrument and not the source? What happened that made it undeniable?

Lesson

How did that realization change the way you lead and the way you pray?

Scripture

What Scripture speaks to the truth that we are instruments and not the source?

Idea 16
Greatest Lessons Come Through Struggles

The storms and failures in your life are not the enemy of hope. They're the classroom where hope is forged, because you learn that God is faithful even when everything falls apart.

Story

What's the hardest season you've been through? What were you facing, and what did it feel like?

Lesson

What did you learn about God in that season that you couldn't have learned any other way?

Scripture

What passage or biblical story about struggles producing something good speaks to your life?

Idea 17
No One Outlives Their Usefulness to God

The lie that you're too old, too tired, or past your prime is the enemy of hope. God has purpose for you as long as you have breath.

Story

Have you ever believed the lie that you've outlived your usefulness? What was happening, and what pulled you out of it?

Lesson

What would you say to someone who believes that lie right now?

Scripture

What Scripture or biblical example shows God using someone others would have counted out?

Idea 18
Little Is Much When God Is in It

The small, unknown, overlooked place you're called to is exactly where God wants to do something significant, and believing that is the definition of living with unexplainable hope.

Story

Why do you want to start at the 40-member church in Western Oklahoma instead of the big stage? What have you seen God do in small places?

Lesson

What have you learned about God in small places that you never learned in big ones?

Scripture

What passage or biblical story captures the idea that little is much when God is in it?
